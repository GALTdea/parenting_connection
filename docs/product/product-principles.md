# Product Principles

## 1. Connection Before Content

The app should help parents connect with their children, not simply produce more entries, reports, or summaries.

## 2. Memories Are The Core Artifact

Every feature should protect the integrity of the memory archive. Original parent-authored text, recordings, dates, and context should remain accessible even when AI features fail or change.

## 3. Gentle, Non-Clinical AI

AI may summarize, organize, and reflect. It must not diagnose, label, score, or predict a child's development, personality, mental health, or family relationship quality.

Use language like:

- "You might notice..."
- "This could be a moment to remember..."
- "A gentle theme across these memories is..."

Avoid language like:

- "Your child is..."
- "This indicates..."
- "Your parenting score..."
- "Developmental concern..."

## 4. Parent Review And Control

AI-assisted text should be reviewed by the parent before it is saved as a final artifact, sent, exported, or shared.

## 5. Privacy Is A Feature

The app handles sensitive child and family data. Collect less data when possible, protect what is collected, and make future deletion/export paths part of architecture planning.

## 6. Mobile Is A First-Class Context

Parents may capture memories in short windows of time. Flows should be fast, touch-friendly, and compatible with an eventual Hotwire Native iOS/Android wrapper.

## 7. Rails Conventions Carry The Product

Prefer understandable Rails conventions over clever architecture. Future AI agents and human developers should be able to infer behavior from normal Rails structure.

## 8. Durable Docs Over Chat Memory

Important decisions, constraints, and feature intent belong in the repo. AI sessions can help generate thinking, but the docs remain the source of truth.
