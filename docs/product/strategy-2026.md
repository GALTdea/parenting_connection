# Product Strategy 2026

## North Star

Does this help parents connect with their children, understand who they are becoming, or preserve meaningful memories?

## Product Thesis

Parents want to remember more than milestones. They want to notice who their children are becoming: the funny phrases, changing interests, hard questions, small rituals, and everyday moments that are easy to lose.

The Parent-Child Connection App should make it simple to collect those moments, reflect on them gently, and turn them into a living archive.

## Primary Audience

The first audience is parents or guardians who want a private, low-friction way to:

- Capture meaningful moments.
- Answer daily prompts.
- Record their child's words or voice.
- Revisit memories over time.
- Receive gentle summaries and reflection letters.

## 2026 Bets

- Parents will use a small daily habit if it feels emotionally rewarding rather than administrative.
- Text and voice capture together create a richer memory archive than text alone.
- AI is most valuable when it helps parents reflect, summarize, and rediscover memories rather than telling them what their child means.
- Trust is a product feature. Privacy, parent review, and respectful language are central to adoption.
- A Rails + Hotwire app can deliver the MVP quickly while preserving a path to native mobile wrappers.

## Strategic Priorities

1. Build the private parent-child memory loop.
2. Make daily capture fast and emotionally meaningful.
3. Preserve memories in a timeline and archive.
4. Add AI summaries only where they deepen reflection and remain parent-reviewed.
5. Keep the architecture compatible with Hotwire Native.

## Non-Goals For The MVP

- Social networking
- Public child profiles
- Clinical analysis
- Behavioral diagnosis
- School, therapy, or medical record management
- Complex co-parenting workflows
- Marketplace or content library
- Fully native mobile apps before the Rails MVP proves the core loop

## Product Risks

- The app could feel like homework if prompts are too demanding.
- AI output could feel presumptive or invasive if not carefully constrained.
- Parents may not trust the app unless privacy and deletion expectations are clear.
- Voice data increases sensitivity and storage complexity.
- The product can drift into productivity software unless the north star remains visible.
