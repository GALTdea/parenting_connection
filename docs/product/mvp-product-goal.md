MVP Product Goal

Goal

Ship a private, Rails-first MVP that helps parents build a meaningful daily conversation ritual with their children, preserve text and voice memories, review those memories over time, and receive gentle AI-assisted reflections.

The MVP should prove whether a simple daily ritual can help parents feel more connected to their children and more aware of who their children are becoming.

Core MVP Test

Will parents consistently use a low-friction daily conversation ritual if the reward is a growing private archive of memories and occasional gentle reflections?

MVP Scope

The MVP includes:

* Parent accounts
* Child profiles
* Daily question system
* Prompt library
* Text responses
* Voice recordings
* Timeline view
* Memory archive
* Basic AI summaries
* Monthly reflection letter

Primary Loop

1. Parent signs in.
2. Parent selects or creates a child profile.
3. Parent receives or chooses a daily question.
4. Parent captures the child’s response with text, voice, or both.
5. The response becomes part of the child’s private timeline.
6. Parent can revisit the memory archive.
7. AI helps summarize recurring themes, memorable moments, and possible reflection points.
8. Parent reviews AI-assisted content before saving, sending, or sharing.

What the MVP Must Feel Like

The product should feel like:

* A quiet daily ritual
* A private family memory archive
* A tool for noticing and understanding a child more deeply
* A gentle assistant, not an evaluator

The product should not feel like:

* A parenting dashboard
* A child assessment tool
* A behavior tracker
* A productivity app
* A clinical, educational, or diagnostic system

Success Definition

The MVP succeeds if a parent can consistently preserve meaningful child memories with low friction and feel that the app helps them notice, understand, and connect with their child more deeply over time.

MVP Quality Bar

* Clear account and profile ownership
* Simple, mobile-friendly capture flow
* Private-by-default memory archive
* Gentle product copy
* Parent-reviewed AI summaries
* No diagnostic language
* No labels, scores, graphs, or rankings for children
* No unnecessary collection of child data
* No AI-generated claims presented as objective truth
* AI output must be framed as draft reflection, not authority

AI Boundaries

AI may help with:

* Summarizing memories
* Identifying recurring interests, themes, and moments
* Drafting monthly reflection letters
* Helping parents notice patterns in the archive
* Making memories easier to revisit

AI must not:

* Diagnose the child
* Label the child’s personality
* Score emotional, behavioral, or developmental traits
* Recommend clinical, educational, or behavioral interventions
* Present interpretations as certain
* Replace parent judgment

Out Of Scope Until After MVP

* Temperament profiles
* Personality graphs
* Advanced personalization
* Gifting
* Printed books
* Multiple family members
* Grandparent accounts
* Educational recommendations
* Child developmental assessments
* Social features
* Multi-household permissions
* Sharing outside the parent account
* Public links
* Paid subscriptions
* Native-only features
* Complex AI personalization
* Clinical, educational, or behavioral recommendations
